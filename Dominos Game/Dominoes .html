Dominoes Game

How to Play

This is a simple Dominoes game for two players. The goal is to be the first to play all your tiles by matching them to the ends of the chain on the table.

  • Setup: 28 domino tiles (0-0 to 6-6). Each player gets 10 tiles, 8 remain in the boneyard.
  • Starting: Random player starts by playing any tile.
  • Playing: Match one half of your tile to the head (left end) or tail (right end) of the chain. Click on a playable tile (highlighted in red) to play it. If both ends match, choose where to place.
  • Drawing: If no playable tile, click "Draw from Boneyard" to draw until you get a playable one or boneyard is empty (then pass).
  • Scoring:
    • If a player runs out of dominoes, they win the round and score points equal to the total pips in their opponent's hand.
    • If the game is blocked and no one can play, the player with the lowest total pips in their hand wins the round.
  • Winning the Game: The first player to reach the agreed-upon target score (e.g., 100 or 150 points) over multiple rounds wins the game.
  • Vs Computer: You are Player 1, computer is Player 2. Computer plays automatically.
  • 2 Players: Take turns on the same device. The current player's hand is visible, while the other player's hand is shown face-down to indicate remaining piece count.
